Last night the Castlebar Mitchels Junior team started another year of competitive football with a home match against Westport.
I was picked at centre back where I haven't played for a really long time, so I was nervous heading out.
To add to the nerves I was picked as captain for the game too!
That was some honour, I've only been playing with the club for a year really and to think that the managers thought I was good enough to be named captain for even one match surprised me.

![Captains](/images/2026-04-18-starting-another-football-season/01_captains.jpg)

The weather had been pretty wet during the day but the rain held off for the match.
However there was a strong wind, and after losing the toss we played with it in the first half.

![Team](/images/2026-04-18-starting-another-football-season/02_team.jpg)

We had a decent half, scoring a few two pointers.
I was nicely involved even if I did give away the ball from a long kick pass at one stage.
We played part of the half with an extra man after a Westport player was black-carded for a body check.
Half time came around with us in the lead 0-9 to 1-2.

In the second half, I fully expected Westport to push on hard and reel us in quickly, they got the first point but we quickly hit back.
Then we got a few more.
Westport seemed to have lost their shape and didn't have the same movement that they had in the first half.

For the last quarter, I ended up drifting in to full back, marking a guy that was about a head taller than me.
Of course the high balls rained in...
I was maybe a bit lucky in one instance that could have been seen as a black card by another ref, but it ended up as only a free.
Then there was a harsh enough penalty as the ref said I was holding an arm as another high ball came in.

In the end, we rode our luck and finished up winning 0-15 to 1-5
